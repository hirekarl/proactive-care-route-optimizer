# Landing Page — Audio Script

Seven beats of spoken narration to accompany the elevator animation on the landing page.
Each beat maps to one visual moment: the initial hero view, then one card per floor as the
camera descends from Floor 6 to Floor 1.

Tone: calm, grounded, urgent without being alarmist. This is a tool for people doing
difficult, essential work — the narration should honor that.

---

## Beat 0 — Hero (initial load) · ~16 sec

*The 3D elevator model orbits slowly in darkness. The logo fades in. Tagline is visible.*

> "Every day, tens of thousands of New York City seniors rely on home-delivered care.
> And every day, their caregivers navigate a city where elevators fail without warning —
> leaving workers stranded and seniors without food. This tool changes that."

---

## Beat 1 — Floor 6: Dispatcher Dashboard · ~13 sec

*Camera swings to the blue card. The elevator cabin rests near the top of the shaft.*

> "The Dashboard surfaces every active risk in one place: at-risk stops, complaints by
> borough, heat advisories. Everything a dispatcher needs to act before a worker reaches
> the lobby."

---

## Beat 2 — Floor 5: Outage Map · ~14 sec

*Camera swings left and slightly down to the violet card. The cabin descends one level.*

> "The Outage Map lays live elevator complaints over active senior-care routes. Each outage
> carries a half-mile proximity ring — so dispatchers can see at a glance which stops are
> inside the danger zone."

---

## Beat 3 — Floor 4: DOB Complaints Feed · ~14 sec

*Camera swings right to the orange card. The cabin descends another level.*

> "The Complaints Feed pulls live from NYC's Department of Buildings open data — filtered
> and flagged for what matters most: chronic offenders, and single-elevator buildings where
> any outage means total inaccessibility."

---

## Beat 4 — Floor 3: Providers Directory · ~13 sec

*Camera swings left to the green card. The cabin continues its descent.*

> "The Providers Directory maps every DFTA-contracted care provider in the five boroughs —
> location, client count, assigned routes. When a building goes dark, dispatchers can find
> the nearest available provider in seconds."

---

## Beat 5 — Floor 2: Elevator Advocate · ~12 sec

*Camera swings right to the pink card. The cabin is near the bottom of the shaft.*

> "Elevator Advocate is built for tenants, organizers, and care teams tracking elevator
> accountability from the outside. If that's your work, this is where to go."

---

## Beat 6 — Floor 1: Senior-Care EDA · ~15 sec

*Camera swings left to the blue card. The cabin arrives at the ground floor.*

> "The Senior-Care Exploratory Analysis is the research behind this tool — the 79%
> proximity rate, the heat-week complaint spike, the Bronx concentration, the 135
> single-elevator buildings. To understand where the numbers come from, start here."

---

## Production notes

- Target read pace: roughly 130–150 words per minute, with a half-beat pause between beats.
- Each beat should feel complete on its own — a visitor who scrolls slowly should hear a
  coherent thought arrive and resolve before the camera moves again.
- Beat 0 runs while the elevator orbits; no scroll is required. Beats 1–6 are triggered by
  scroll position as the camera locks onto each floor card.
- If a beat plays while the camera is still traveling between floors, the narration should
  already be fading out — audio leads the animation, not the other way around.
- Word counts per beat (approximate): 0: 38 words · 1: 30 · 2: 32 · 3: 33 · 4: 31 · 5: 27 · 6: 35
